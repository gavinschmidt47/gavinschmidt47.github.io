VAC-ation Denied

An Interactive Study of Steam's Most Dedicated Cheaters and the Games They Loved

🔍 Personal Research • 🗄️ 3,993 Banned Accounts • 🎮 1,312 Games Analyzed

Research Overview

Valve's anti-cheat infrastructure has banned hundreds of thousands of Steam accounts. But who were these players, and what were they actually doing? This project analyzes a dataset of 3,993 banned accounts across 1,312 games to answer the questions VAC never bothers explaining: which games produce the most cheaters, do high-hour players cheat more, when did ban waves cluster, and what does a "repeat offender" profile actually look like?

All visualizations below are fully interactive. Zoom, pan, hover for details, and filter by ban type. The data comes from Steam's public API and covers accounts with VAC bans, developer game bans, or both — spanning bans issued from the early 2000s through 2025.

Methodology Notes

All data was sourced from Steam's public API. Accounts are represented by anonymized row identifiers — no usernames, profile links, or personally identifiable information appear in any visualization. The dataset covers VAC bans and developer-issued game bans. Community bans, trade bans, and private-profile accounts are excluded or appear with zero-value playtime. DaysSinceLastBan represents elapsed days at time of dataset collection (approximately March 2026), which is used to approximate calendar ban year. Playtime figures are total Steam library hours, not per-game hours, as Steam's API returns aggregate playtime for most banned accounts. The 2,783 accounts showing zero playtime are likely private profiles or accounts that cleared their history.
